Quaid e Azam Mohammad Ali Jinnah

Founder Of Pakistan



Early Life

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah was born on December 25, 1876 (or possibly October 20, 1875) in Karachi, which was then part of British-controlled India.He was born into a prosperous Muslim merchant family and was the eldest of seven children.Jinnah was initially educated at the Sindh Madrassat-ul-Islam and the Christian Mission School in Karachi

Political Career

Jinnah formally entered politics in 1905 from the platform of the Indian National Congress. He went to England that year along with Gopal Krishna Gokhale as a member of a Congress delegation to plead the cause of Indian self-government during the British elections. A year later, he served as Secretary to Dadabhai Naoroji, the then Indian National Congress President

Legacy

Jinnah's unwavering leadership and determination were instrumental in the creation of Pakistan as a separate homeland for the Muslims of the Indian subcontinent. He is revered as the "Quaid-e-Azam" (Great Leader) who achieved this historic feat.
